What it does

Takes a PRD or feature spec and produces a customer-facing product brief for sales, CS, and marketing. Creates the brief as a Notion page in the Product briefs for GTM Repo, links it back to the source PRD, and iterates on your feedback.

Workflow

1 Fetch the PRD — reads the source Notion page to understand the feature
2 Check format — fetches a recent GTM brief from the repo for consistency
3 Generate the brief — translates engineering language into sales-ready content
4 Create in Notion — publishes under the GTM Repo, links back to the PRD
5 Iterate — refine based on your feedback until it's ready to share

Brief structure

Feature Name & Eligibility
Value Proposition
Problems We Solve
How We Solve It
How It Works
FAQ
GTM Talking Points
Competitive Positioning
Suggested Customer Messaging
GTM Collateral Checklist

Translation rules

These rules ensure the brief reads well for salespeople and CS, not engineers:

No internal jargon — "PLG flows" becomes "built-in invite surfaces." No acronyms without explanation.

Customer-facing feature name — use the name users see in the UI, not the internal project name.

Benefits over implementation — "admins get full control" not "backend guard on prepaidAccounts."

External segment names — "enterprise customers" not "Direct Sales customers."

Name requesting customers — include them in Problems We Solve and collateral checklist for targeted outreach.

Self-serve when possible — link to the settings page, don't say "reach out to us."

Renewal/upsell framing — explicitly call out which plans don't have the feature and frame as a renewal lever.

Pyramid principle — lead with the takeaway. Details and supporting evidence below.
